]]> The balcony of Saint Peter’s Basilica is where the Pope is presented to the people of the world after the conclave. It is the one above the main door, with the red color on the walls on both sides. When a new Pope is elected the gathering of cardinals is called a conclave. Conclave comes from the latin Cum Clave, which means with key. The cardinals are locked in the Sistine Chapel together, when they choose a new Pope from their midst. After a new Pope is elected, he will be presented to the people on this main balcony on Saint Peterâ€™s.

It is also from this balcony the Pope blesses the city and the world every Easter, the Urbi et Orbi. Under the balcony there is a carving which shows Jesus handing the keys to heaven to Saint Peter.
